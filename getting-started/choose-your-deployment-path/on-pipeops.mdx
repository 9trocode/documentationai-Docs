## Provision a server on PipeOps

Use PipeOps-managed hosting to spin up a server on your preferred cloud provider, pay in local currency, and get to your project deployment quickly.

<Callout kind="info">

You complete hosting setup once per workspace. After you provision a server on PipeOps, you can reuse it for multiple projects.

</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose On PipeOps hosting" icon="cloud">

From the server creation flow, select **On PipeOps** as your hosting option. This tells PipeOps to provision and manage infrastructure on your behalf.

<Image
  src="https://docs.pipeops.io/assets/images/provision-server-on-pipeops-7b9e0f4a0b06a902024a0e83389d5f66.png"
  alt="Server creation screen with On PipeOps hosting option selected"
  width="1280"
  height="720"
/>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick a provider, region, and pricing model" icon="globe">

Choose your preferred cloud provider, such as **Digital Ocean**, **Google Cloud**, **AWS**, **Azure**, or **Huawei Cloud**. Then select a region that aligns with your users or compliance requirements.

PipeOps uses a geo-location pricing model and bills in your **local currency** based on the region you choose. Review the estimated monthly cost before you continue.

<Image
  src="https://docs.pipeops.io/assets/images/cost-estimator-26f5a61dfb194eca9489e4b51f83a51b.png"
  alt="Cost estimator showing region, provider selection, and pricing breakdown"
  width="1280"
  height="720"
/>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Adjust CPU, memory, and storage" icon="settings">

Use the sliders to configure your server resources:

- **CPU** for processing capacity
- **Memory** for runtime performance
- **Storage** for databases, logs, and assets

As you move the sliders, the **cost estimator** updates to show an expected monthly price for your configuration.

<Callout kind="tip">

The cost estimator shows an estimate based on current pricing for your chosen provider and region. Actual billing may vary slightly due to provider pricing changes, taxes, or currency fluctuations.

</Callout>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a plan" icon="bar-chart">

Choose a hosting plan that matches your expected usage:

- **Growth** for smaller workloads and staging environments
- **Scale** for higher traffic or production workloads
- **Custom** for specialized or large-scale needs

You can switch between **monthly** and **yearly** billing for each plan. For detailed limits and feature comparisons, see the pricing page (search for "PipeOps pricing" in your browser if it is not linked from this doc set).

<Image
  src="https://docs.pipeops.io/assets/images/plan-modal-047e28da85c8e8daa3f09b756ff3aac8.png"
  alt="Plan selection modal with Growth, Scale, and Custom plan options"
  width="1280"
  height="720"
/>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a payment method" icon="credit-card">

Confirm your hosting selection and choose a payment provider. PipeOps supports:

- **Stripe** for global card payments and wallets
- **Paystack** for supported African markets and local payment methods

After you choose a provider, PipeOps redirects you to a secure checkout page where you complete payment authorization.

<Image
  src="https://docs.pipeops.io/assets/images/payment-modal-f021c173c754581b2c21f1c103002c41.png"
  alt="Payment method modal showing Stripe and Paystack options"
  width="1280"
  height="720"
/>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Access your dashboard" icon="check-circle">

Once payment succeeds, PipeOps confirms your subscription and provisions your managed server. You see a pop-up notification, and your hosting dashboard becomes available with your new environment.

<Image
  src="https://docs.pipeops.io/assets/images/welcome-dashboard-5d6a9ae6713536c15784c3b8edb0f043.png"
  alt="Welcome dashboard screen shown after successful server provisioning"
  width="1280"
  height="720"
/>

Use the dashboard to monitor usage, manage resources, and configure deployments.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Continue to project deployment" icon="rocket">

With your PipeOps-managed server ready, proceed to deploy your application. Follow the project deployment guide for step-by-step instructions (search for "PipeOps project deployment guide" if it is not yet available in this doc set).

  </Step>
</Steps>
